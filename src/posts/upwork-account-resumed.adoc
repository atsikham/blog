= Success story: how my Upwork account was resumed after 4 years of suspension

I've read plenty of LinkedIn posts about Upwork suspensions that ended nowhere — no response, no reinstatement, just silence.
I wanted to share my case because the outcome was different, and I think it's worth knowing that getting your account back is sometimes possible.

== How it started

In March 2022, Upwork announced they were suspending accounts for freelancers based in Russia and Belarus.
By that point I hadn't been using the account for a while anyway, so I didn't really care.
I received the standard email:

[source]
----
On March 7th, we made the difficult decision to suspend business operations in Russia and Belarus.
As of today, we've suspended access to your Upwork account because our records indicate you are located in one of these countries.
If and when you relocate to a region where we operate, we'll be happy to review your account for reinstatement.
In the meantime, we're taking measures to ensure that the reputation you've built on our site, including customer feedback and your Job Success Score, remains intact.

...Our support team members are also here to help. For more information, please visit support-appeals@upwork.com.
----

The key sentence there was: _"If and when you relocate to a region where we operate, we'll be happy to review your account for reinstatement."_

I held onto that.

== Two years later — the support maze

In 2024 I decided to try to get the account back.
The first thing I did was email support-appeals@upwork.com, as the suspension notice had instructed.
The reply started with:

[source]
----
Hello, This email address is not monitored.
----

Not a great sign.

They pointed me to an appeal form instead.
I filled it out.
The response I got back was confusing:

[source]
----
We appreciate you reaching out to us with your appeal concerning the suspension of your Upwork account.
Upon diligent review of your case, we have determined that the account you referenced is either not found within our database or is active without any suspensions noted.
This discrepancy might be due to inadvertent errors in the account details provided or a misapprehension regarding the account's current standing...
----

The account wasn't active — I had a clear suspension message every time I tried to log in.
Something was clearly broken on their end.

By that point the support experience had changed significantly from what it used to be:

* Live chat agents are gone, replaced by a bot that mostly goes in circles
* Support tickets can only be created by authenticated users, and standard freelancer plan doesn't allow that
* The appeals email address isn't monitored

I kept filling out the appeal form.
Same result every time.
Later, after the account was eventually reinstated, I could see in my profile that every single appeal had been logged — they just hadn't been acted on.

== What finally worked

As a last attempt I wrote to support@upwork.com directly.

I'm not sure whether it was that email or the cumulative weight of all those appeal submissions, but a month or two later I got a message asking me to provide updated personal details and documents.

There was one more twist.
Years earlier, two of my accounts had been merged into one to comply with Upwork's one-account policy.
After reviewing the documentation I submitted, they resumed only one of the two merged accounts — the older one, which had no recent work history, ratings, or feedback.

So I spent more time going back and forth with support to get the account with actual history restored.
Eventually that worked too.

== What I'd tell someone in the same situation

If your account is suspended and you're going in circles with appeals, a few things that might help:

* *Keep submitting the appeal form.* It feels pointless when you're getting identical rejection messages, but the submissions are logged. Volume seems to matter eventually.
* *Email support@upwork.com directly.* The dedicated appeals address is unmonitored. The main support address still appears to reach real people.
* *Be patient but persistent.* My case took years of repeated attempts before anything moved.
* *Don't give up early.* The automated responses make it feel like there's no human on the other side, but there is.
